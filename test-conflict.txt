I began my software journey with a strong curiosity about how applications
 work behind the scenes. What started as simple experimentation gradually turned 
 into a focused pursuit of building real-world systems. I was especially drawn to backend development, 
 where logic, performance, and architecture come together to create meaningful impact.

 Early on, I chose to work with Laravel as my primary backend framework. Its elegant structure 
 and powerful features helped me understand core concepts like MVC architecture, routing, and database 
 management. Alongside it, I explored PostgreSQL to gain a deeper understanding of relational databases and 
 how to design scalable data models.

As I progressed, I didn’t just focus on writing code—I focused on understanding systems. I became 
interested in how applications handle real-world complexity: queues, background jobs, caching, and 
event-driven architectures. Redis became an important part of my stack as I explored performance 
optimization and asynchronous processing.

On the frontend, I started working with React.js to build interactive user interfaces. This allowed me to 
connect the backend logic with user experience, creating full-stack applications. I learned how to manage state, 
build reusable components, and structure applications in a clean, maintainable way.

One of the key turning points in my journey was working on industry-level projects. Instead of small tutorials,
 I started building real SaaS-style applications like analytics dashboards, subscription trackers, and expense management 
 systems. These projects pushed me to think about scalability, user experience, and production-level challenges.

I also gained experience integrating with third-party platforms, especially in the e-commerce space. Working with APIs, 
handling webhooks, and ensuring secure communication (like HMAC verification) helped me understand how distributed systems 
interact with each other in real-world environments.

Debugging and problem-solving became a core part of my daily work. Whether it was fixing complex backend 
issues, optimizing database queries, or resolving integration bugs, I learned how to approach problems systematically. 
Each challenge improved my ability to think critically and design better solutions.

Over time, I developed a strong interest in system design. I started thinking beyond individual features and 
began focusing on how entire systems are structured—how data flows, how services communicate, and how performance 
can be optimized at scale. This mindset helped me grow from a developer into someone who can design robust applications.
